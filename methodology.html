Methodology & About

How this wiki works: sourcing standards, reliability labels, editorial policy, and what this project is (and isn't).

What Is This?

The Personfu Arizona Public Intel Wiki is an open-source intelligence (OSINT) aggregation and research project built under the FLLC design system.

It collects, organizes, and presents publicly available information about Arizona's government operations, cybersecurity landscape, defense installations, border infrastructure, water politics, and historical anomalies.

What It Is

  • An educational research tool
  • A public records aggregator
  • A FOIA/declassification document vault
  • An OSINT methodology demonstration

What It Is Not

  • Not affiliated with any government agency
  • Not a source of classified information
  • Not a news organization
  • Not a conspiracy theory platform

Sourcing Standards

Source Hierarchy

  1. Primary government sources — Official .gov websites, RSS feeds, press releases, court records
  2. FOIA releases — Documents obtained through Freedom of Information Act requests
  3. Declassified documents — CIA Reading Room, National Archives, agency declassification programs
  4. Congressional records — CRS reports, hearing transcripts, GAO audits
  5. Credible journalism — Established outlets with editorial standards; always secondary to primary sources
  6. Academic research — Peer-reviewed publications and university research
  7. Witness testimony — Named individuals with documented accounts; labeled accordingly

What We Don't Use

  • Anonymous social media posts without corroboration
  • Paywalled content that can't be verified
  • Classified or leaked materials
  • Sources that cannot be independently checked
Technical Architecture

Data Pipeline

Automated Python scripts run daily via GitHub Actions, collecting from:

  • CISA — Advisories and Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog
  • FBI — Cyber division press releases
  • DOJ — Cybercrime and national security press
  • DHS — Department-wide news and alerts
  • NWS — National Weather Service API for Arizona zones
  • FAA/NOTAM — Aviation alerts and restrictions
  • .gov Registry — Full domain catalog (15,000+ entries)
  • AZ511 — 44 live traffic camera feeds

Technology

Static site hosted on GitHub Pages. No server-side processing. All data is pre-fetched JSON. Frontend is vanilla HTML/CSS/JS with Leaflet for maps and Canvas for charts.
